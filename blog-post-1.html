How to set your project up for success

Hello! My name is Tony and I graduated from DigitalCrafts, a full stack web development bootcamp. Being in a bootcamp is no joke üòì, it is fast-paced and that is what I want to cover today.

Research üìñ

No matter how 'limited' of time you may have, always do your research! This consists of interface designs, frameworks or even guides on how to accomplish a feature. Of course you should be mindful of how long your research is but anything that you may find helpful, save it in a note. This will cut down the time when you have to look something up.

Throughout the projects that I have worked on at DigitalCrafts, here a few things you may want to consider:

  • user interface
  • database modeling
  • helpful frameworks/libraries
  • feature implementation

Organize üìö

Okay so you've done your research, now what? Take that note where you wrote down everything and start filling in your project board! Github has a nice feature where you're able to create a project board for an existing repository. If you're unsure of how to start one, here is a guide.

The point of constructing this kanban is that your team can add their own resources and now the entire team will be able to have access to helpful articles or videos. When filling out a card for a column, be succinct yet descriptive of the resource. Final thoughts üß†

I highly recommend this process as it also helps guide productive conversations such as 'What features should we include?', 'How will we implement it?' and 'Who is currently working on which feature?' This project board will act as your itinerary while you work through your project. It'll help guide your team to maintain a healthy pace but also to be organized so that you could improve your workflow productivity and efficiency.
